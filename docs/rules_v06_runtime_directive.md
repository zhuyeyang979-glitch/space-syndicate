# v0.6 Runtime Development Directive

## Status and authority

This document turns `docs/tabletop_rulebook_v06.md` into an implementation boundary. It is a development directive, not a claim that v0.6 is already playable.

The atomic sprint order and deletion gates are tracked in `docs/rules_v06_development_plan.md`.

For new gameplay work, the v0.6 rulebook is the semantic authority. v0.5 rules, resources, controllers and tests remain historical/runtime evidence until their owning domain is cut over, but they must not be expanded as the future design.

Do not load Markdown at runtime. Confirmed numbers must eventually move into one v0.6 Ruleset Resource; UI and card text consume typed snapshots and receipts rather than duplicating formulas.

## 1. Delete, replace, advance

### Delete at owning-domain cutover

- Fixed victory depth table `3/90 ... 8/360` and validators that reject dynamic coverage.
- Five project slots, project shares, investment units, project controller and project-attributed GDP.
- Industry-capacity tiers and card-group capacity reservations.
- Legacy contracts, contract responses, violations and project-to-project agreement state.
- Route-card ownership, abstract route HP/damage and route-repair effects.
- Area-derived district HP, city HP bonuses and separate facility current HP.
- Non-unit direct HP damage, including ordinary barrage cards and heat-trigger damage.
- Direct GDP/cash/mana card effects that bypass commodity sale receipts.
- Automatic same-family hand upgrade outside the full-hand commodity exception.
- Financial-distress countdown and emergency hand sale.
- Monster duration refresh semantics.

### Replace

- Fixed victory requirements -> dynamic live-region coverage plus GDP-per-required-region.
- Project economy -> public facilities, installed commodity rates, continuous goods flow and commodity ownership.
- Abstract district damage -> facility-derived regional shared HP and integrity.
- Industry capacity -> six private mana pools generated by the player's own commodity GDP.
- Single-mode route assumptions -> ordered multimodal legs plus a route tag set.
- One-shot product production/demand -> persistent installed production and demand rates.
- Generic counter flow -> direct-player-interaction-only, one-layer response.

### Advance existing ownership boundaries

- `VictoryControlRuntimeController` remains the single owner of control, qualification, audit and outcome receipts, but consumes dynamic region state and commodity GDP.
- `MonsterRuntimeController` and `MilitaryRuntimeController` remain the only gameplay owners allowed to submit shared-HP damage requests.
- Card Inventory remains the single owner of ordinary-hand count, manual merging and the full-hand commodity exception.
- Card Queue/Execution remain the single card commitment and atomic resolution path; obsolete industry reservations are removed with their domain.
- Presentation services continue to render typed public/private snapshots; they never calculate HP, routes, GDP, mana or victory requirements.

## 2. Required runtime state

The concrete Resource and class names may change, but one authority must own the following data.

### Region infrastructure state

```text
region_id
lifecycle_state: active | destroyed
region_revision
current_hp
facilities_by_slot
destroyed_at
revived_at
last_damage_receipt_id
```

`max_hp` is derived from the current facility list. Saving a separately writable `max_hp` is forbidden because it can drift from facility ranks.

### Public facility state

```text
facility_id
region_id
slot_kind
color_or_neutral
owner_player_index | neutral
rank I-IV
installed_products
rent_profile_id
```

There is no facility `current_hp`. UI may display a virtual share derived from the regional integrity, but it is not authoritative state.

### Installed commodity rate

```text
installation_id
commodity_id
color
installer_player_index
direction: production | demand
base_units_per_minute
source_card_rank
facility_id
region_revision
```

An installation persists until its facility is destroyed. Repeated and higher-rank commodity cards create additive installations.

Concrete demand installed in a market also owns a commodity-specific demand
record in `CommodityFlowRuntimeController`, keyed by
`market_facility_id + commodity_id`. That record contains steady demand,
unmet backlog, recovery budget/remainders, cap and revision. A bare market
without a concrete commodity installation does not create high demand.

### Route snapshot

```text
route_id
commodity_id
source_region_id
market_region_id
ordered_legs
mode_tags
facility_ids
shortest_legal_distance
bottleneck_units_per_minute
expected_rents
region_revision_fingerprint
```

`mode_tags` is a set. A route containing one sea leg satisfies a sea condition, and a route containing both air and land can satisfy either condition while the same goods are counted once per card.

### Commodity sale receipt

```text
receipt_id
commodity_owner
commodity_id
color
units
source_region_id
market_region_id
route_id
gross_value
rent_rows
owner_net_cash
gdp_value
settled_at
```

One sold unit produces one settlement receipt. Facilities, warehouses, orders and mana must consume this receipt instead of creating parallel GDP.

### Player mana state

Store six balances per player. Recovery is derived from that player's own color-attributed commodity sale receipts in the configured observation window. Commodity cards bypass mana cost; all other played cards declare colored and/or generic mana costs.

### Commodity belt visibility snapshot

The global belt owner stores the full ordered card track. A viewer-scoped service derives:

```text
visibility_revision
viewer_player_index
own_recent_commodity_gdp_per_minute
gdp_tier_index
gdp_tier_count
visible_suffix_count
visible_card_ids
obscured_cards: card_position + color_only
next_rank_refresh_at
```

GDP tiers are formed only from each active player's recent owned-commodity GDP total. Equal values share one tier. If there is only one tier, every player sees the full belt. Otherwise the lowest tier sees the full belt and the highest tier sees the configured minimum suffix.

Cards outside the clear suffix remain present as redacted records containing only position/motion and commodity color. They must not contain card ID, exact commodity, rank, name key, art key, effect data, tooltip text or accessible text capable of reconstructing those fields. Claim validation must include the viewer and `visibility_revision`; only IDs in `visible_card_ids` are legal. UI blur is presentation, not security authority.

## 3. Atomic event order

For the same authoritative timestamp, use this order:

1. Accept and lock player/card/unit intents.
2. Resolve card costs, construction, upgrade and repair.
3. Resolve monster and military movement/attacks that were locked before the timestamp.
4. Commit shared-HP damage, region destruction/revival and `region_revision` changes.
5. Rebuild route snapshots and effective facility rates from regional integrity.
6. Advance continuous production, demand, storage and transport.
7. Create sale receipts; settle cash, rents, GDP and mana recovery inputs.
8. Resolve storage fees and bankruptcy.
9. Refresh due commodity-belt GDP tiers and viewer visibility snapshots.
10. Recalculate live-region count, control, qualification and audit outcome.
11. Publish one ordered receipt batch to presentation, AI, save and replay consumers.

Victory must not resolve before already-locked same-timestamp damage or reconstruction. A region-destruction receipt is idempotent and contains pre/post HP, facilities destroyed, region count before/after and victory requirement before/after.

## 4. Continuous economy rules

- Store authored production/demand as rates per minute; integrate with fixed-point arithmetic over time.
- Compute each installed product's effective rate as `product_base_rate * min(1, facility_rank_capacity / all_installed_base_rate) * regional_integrity`; perform the proportional facility-capacity limit before route allocation.
- Do not evict existing warehouse contents merely because integrity lowers intake capacity; block/reroute new overflow. Destroy all local contents only when the region reaches zero HP.
- For every commodity and authoritative tick, allocate in this order: concrete
  market steady demand and permitted backlog recovery; non-accumulating
  same-region/direct-adjacent-land ambient consumption using fresh output
  only; matching reachable warehouse storage; then waste.
- Every surviving non-ruin region has a low data-driven ambient demand for
  every active commodity. Unfulfilled ambient demand expires at tick end and
  never becomes market backlog.
- Concrete market demand continues to accrue commodity-specific unmet backlog
  when supply or a legal route is unavailable. Current steady demand is
  fulfilled before old backlog; only extra delivery may recover backlog.
- Warehouse inventory can satisfy concrete market demand but must never be
  drained by ambient consumption.
- Continuous output that cannot be sold or stored becomes non-saleable waste
  and disappears. It creates no Sale Receipt, cash, GDP, asset or rent. Legacy
  backpressure may migrate once into historical waste totals and must never
  become inventory.
- Aggregate demand at a bottleneck before proportional allocation. Never allocate by array iteration order.
- Route selection optimizes expected commodity-owner net cash under current legal capacity, then uses arrival time, transfer count and stable route ID as tie breakers.
- Lock distance premium to shortest legal economic distance. Later detours do not increase price.
- Multimodal condition matching reads the route tag set; one shipment contributes at most once to one card resolution.

## 5. Positive-feedback guardrails

Do not add a second hidden comeback currency. Preserve these explicit brakes:

- Facility rank/capacity ceilings.
- Both production and demand are required for sale.
- Route bottlenecks and rent drain.
- Storage cost and loss of overflow.
- Non-commodity mana costs scale with persistence, range and disruption.
- The shared card window is 30 seconds: 20 seconds planning, 5 seconds public
  card-order bidding and 5 seconds lock. The first three windows use the
  authored 45/35/5/5 opening cadence. Ordinary capacity is one card; only an
  authoritative organization capability may raise it, to a hard maximum of
  three. Belt claims remain outside the ordinary-card count.
- Regional integrity scales all facility efficiency.
- Monster target scoring gives material weight to high-GDP, high-flow and storage-concentrated regions.
- Audit reveals leading economic assets for counterplay.
- The commodity belt gives lower-GDP tiers a longer look-ahead window while leaders see only the near-expiry suffix.
- Monster battles expose cash to risk and distribute the neutral public pool, but do not grant GDP or mana.

Balance tests must measure at least: GDP share by leader, mana income by color, card plays per minute, facility integrity, route congestion, warehouse overflow, monster pressure by player rank, wager cash transfer and time-to-recover after a region loss.

## 6. Player-text requirements

Player text uses these terms consistently:

- `区域完整度`, not facility individual HP.
- `基础生产率` and `基础需求率` for permanent installed commodity cards.
- `有效生产率/有效需求率/有效吞吐` after integrity scaling.
- `存续区域` and `废墟区域` for the dynamic victory denominator.
- `路线包含海运/空运/陆运`, not “全程海运/空运/陆运” unless a future card explicitly requires it.
- `购买费用` for cash paid to acquire a non-commodity card; `打出费用` for mana paid when playing it.
- Commodity cards explicitly say `免费领取；免费打出`.
- The belt describes `清晰区长度` as a private ranking clue. Obscured cards expose only color and motion; tooltip, accessibility text, controller focus and logs must say only such as `模糊的蓝色商品牌，尚不可领取`, never the exact commodity or rank.

Card text must never say “对一座设施造成 X 点生命伤害”. Unit attacks say “对目标区域共享生命造成 X 点伤害”. Non-unit disruption says exactly which effective rate, capacity, rent or availability changes and for how long.

## 7. Migration work order

1. Freeze a recoverable v0.5 snapshot and mark its rules/resources as historical runtime evidence.
2. Add the v0.6 Ruleset Resource and region-infrastructure authority without routing production gameplay through it yet.
3. Cut over facilities, installed commodity rates and shared regional HP as one domain; delete area/city HP writers in the same change.
4. Cut over continuous production, concrete market backlog, ambient regional
   consumption, multimodal routing, warehouse buffering, waste and unique
   sale receipts.
5. Cut over commodity GDP and six private mana pools; remove project GDP and industry capacity together.
6. Cut over dynamic victory and audit ordering after region and commodity receipts are authoritative.
7. Migrate inventory, persistent commodity placement, conditional order/supply cards and direct-interaction counters.
8. Add viewer-scoped commodity-belt ranking/visibility and enforce the same filtered input for humans and AI.
9. Cut over monster/military damage, targeting pressure, duration extension and wager rules.
10. Cut over bankruptcy, neutral estates, save envelope, AI and all player-facing text.
11. Delete remaining v0.5 runtime selectors, validators and compatibility fallbacks only after v0.6 conformance tests pass.

## 8. Save and compatibility policy

v0.5 saves do not contain facility ownership/rank, installed product rates, regional revision or commodity sale receipts. v0.6 must start a new game and use a new save schema. Do not fabricate neutral facilities or inferred commodity ownership to continue an old match.

Old saves may remain available to an old build or be preserved as read-only backups. A v0.6 save must not be loaded by v0.5 and must not silently downgrade.

## 9. Minimum conformance gates

- Shared HP derivation, proportional efficiency, construction, upgrade, repair, overkill and total destruction.
- Mixed-owner regions and shared repair benefits.
- Dynamic victory rounding, destruction/revival during qualification and same-timestamp audit endpoint ordering.
- Persistent commodity installations, manual merge and full-hand auto-merge exception.
- Commodity-belt tier ties, all-zero opening state, rank refresh, clear/obscured boundary, color-only redaction, stale-claim rejection, tooltip/accessibility privacy and AI non-cheating.
- Multimodal path legality, tag matching, bottleneck allocation, distance anti-loop and rerouting after destruction.
- One sale receipt conservation across cash, rent, GDP and mana.
- Commodity-free play versus non-commodity mana payment.
- Bankruptcy, neutral facilities, rent-to-wager-pool and region destruction.
- Privacy scopes, audit reveal, save/load idempotency and replay receipt ordering.

No UI screenshot, AI decision or balance report counts as rules evidence unless it consumes the same authoritative snapshots and receipts as gameplay.
